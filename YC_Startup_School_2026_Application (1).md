Most recently, I built **Fineprint**, which is a tool that addresses the ongoing problem of agreeing to terms and conditions thoughtlessly. It is an AI-powered contract analysis platform that gives users plain-English summaries, clause-by-clause risk scores, and actionable checklists before they sign anything.

I shipped it as a full product across three surfaces:
- **Web app** — [fineprint.dev](https://fineprint.dev) — upload PDFs/DOCX files for instant analysis
- **iOS app** — [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/fineprint-ai/id6763127284) — built-in document scanner and on-device OCR so you can snap a photo of a lease or TOS on the spot
- **Chrome extension** — [Chrome Web Store](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/fineprint-terms-of-servic/gannnlbjphlioiglckimemojhceclhle) — auto-detects Terms of Service and Privacy Policies on any webpage and surfaces a risk summary in one click

The whole system includes auth, a saved-analysis dashboard, and a conversational Q&A feature for follow-up questions about your document.

## Hacking a Non-Computer System

I am very big into cars. I love driving everywhere and enjoying scenic views. A year ago, I imported a Diesel German Audi (2014 Audi Q5 3.0L TDI) ([photo](https://imgur.com/a/ltSdNun)) to the United States. That process was very very complicated, but that is not my main story behind this answer;

Very recently I had to renew my registration, while I was in the process of moving to another apartment. Nobody likes to deal with the motor vehicle department in the US, and I am no exception. Thankfully I was eligible to renew my registration online, so that was not an issue. Just fill out the information on the website and have the renewed documents mailed to me. Sounds good, right?

When mailing the new documents to me, the RMV sent everything to my old apartment, which I had long ago moved out of. Easy fix, just order a replacement and eat the fee. Or so I thought..

That replacement never came. My registration expired and I kept getting tickets on top of tickets, even though my registration was valid, due to my sticker not being applied. I tried ordering one more time, but it still did not come. I was stuck waiting on hold for the RMV for 30 minutes almost daily. I was so tired of it. I no longer wanted my sticker. I wanted revenge.

I remembered I am a Software Engineer in this cursed and blessed era. Whenever I have a programming problem, I consult and brainstorm with AI, before even looking at the code. I was making the right API call, just not to the correct client. I thought to myself, if I consult AI on my code errors, why not do it for the real life errors.

Which model would best suit my needs? Grok, of course. I created a new chat, and started marinating the context with dislike over the bureaucratic processes of the government. Then, it was time to shine. My prompt was the following:

> *"\*previous context\* whats the most malicious official thing i can do to fight this, i want to ruin their life"*

The following is what occurred; Over the next week, I sent very mean-worded emails to the State Representative and State Senator. I tried to contact federal officials, but that was too far out of reach. I found the emails of everyone higher up in the RMV, with details to my situation, and full intent to pursue legal action. They folded. I was receiving calls from RMV supervisors daily. I made 3 go to voicemail before answering, putting them on hold, 'losing connection' and then finally talking to them.

They paid all the fines involved with the situation. My sticker was there for less than a week later, sent to the correct address. Matter of fact, they sent 2. No more tickets. I won.
